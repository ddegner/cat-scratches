You're unlikely to find papas guisadas—stewed potatoes—on a restaurant menu. It's a dish you'll find in Mexican and Mexican-American home kitchens, especially on nights when the fridge is running low, and a bag of potatoes is still sitting on the counter. It's simple, filling, and made with ingredients most people already have on hand. It's also a dish my abuela would insist I eat when I had a runny nose, whether I was hungry or not.

At its most basic, papas guisadas are chunks of potato simmered in a brothy tomato sauce. It's often served as a side, but it can easily turn into a full meal with the addition of eggs or meat. For this version, I steer in that heartier direction, simmering the potatoes with a generous amount of pork belly so it can be eaten more like a stew than a side dish.

Although the recipe takes a couple hours to come together, the majority of the time is hands-off while the pork belly tenderizes. As it cooks, its collagen slowly breaks down into gelatin, turning the meat tender while enriching the broth. That broth is the foundation for the sauce, which is blended with fire-roasted tomatoes, garlic, and guajillo chiles. Guajillos bring a mild, fruity, slightly earthy flavor without much heat; if you want more of a kick, a couple of chiles de árbol or chiltepín will do the job. A touch of cumin, oregano, and black peppercorns rounds everything out with warm, smoky depth.

Once blended, the sauce is smooth and still loose, but it thickens as it's tossed with the potatoes. Stirring more aggressively breaks down some of the potatoes' edges, releasing starch and giving the stew a creamier consistency. Leave it mostly undisturbed, and it stays brothier—both approaches work, it just depends on what texture you prefer.

Like many stews, I think this one tastes even better after it's had a little time to sit. The potatoes continue to absorb the sauce, and the flavors settle into something more cohesive by the next day. Finish it with a sprinkle of cilantro and green onions, and it's a comforting, easy meal that works any time of year.

## Papas Guisadas (Mexican Stewed Potatoes)

Prep 15 mins

Cook 2 hrs

Total 2 hrs 15 mins

Serves 4 to 6

Keep Screen Awake

For the Potatoes and Pork:

* 2 pounds (900 g) skinless, boneless pork belly, cut into 2-inch chunks
 
* 1 white onion (8 ounces; 226 g), peeled and halved
 
* 5 medium cloves garlic
 
* 5 cups homemade chicken stock or store-bought low-sodium chicken broth
 
* Kosher salt
 
* 3 pounds (1370 g) russet potatoes (about 6 potatoes), peeled and cut into 1-inch chunks

For the Sauce:

* 1 (14.5-ounce; 411 g) can fire-roasted tomatoes
 
* 3 medium cloves garlic
 
* 2 guajillo chiles (about 12 g), stemmed and seeded, and torn into pieces
 
* 1 teaspoon ground cumin
 
* 1 teaspoon dried oregano
 
* 1/2 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
 
* Cilantro leaves, for garnish
 
* Thinly sliced scallions, for garnish

1. In a large stock pot or Dutch oven, combine pork belly, onion, garlic, stock, and 2 teaspoons salt. Bring to a boil over medium heat, reduce heat to maintain a simmer, and cook, covered, until pork belly can be easily pierced by a fork, about 1 1/2 hours. Add the potatoes and continue to simmer, stirring occasionally, until fork-tender, about 20 minutes.
 
2. Using a spider skimmer or slotted spoon, transfer pork and potatoes to a large bowl. Discard onion and garlic. Skim fat from the broth using a ladle or spoon (or pour through a fat separator and let it sit for 3 minutes to allow fat to float to the top). Reserve 3/4 cup (200 ml) of the broth, and reserve the remaining broth or for another use. Whip pot clean and set aside. Place pork on a cutting board and finely chop; return to bowl.
 
3. **For the Sauce:** In a blender jar, combine tomatoes, garlic, guajillos, cumin, oregano, and black peppercorns. Add the reserved 3/4 cup (200 ml) cooking liquid to the blender. Submerge the dried chiles in the liquid and let sit until chiles are slightly softened, about 5 minutes. Blend, starting at the lowest speed and gradually increasing to high, until smooth, about 1 minute. Season with salt to taste.
 
4. Pour sauce into the now-empty pot, then stir in pork and potatoes. Cook over medium-low heat until warmed through. Stir the stew to help combine ingredients: The more aggressively you stir, the creamier the dish will be (the final consistency is a matter of personal preference), but the sauce should at least be thick enough to coat the potatoes and pork. Adjust consistency with extra water or broth, if desired. Season to taste with salt. Serve, topping with cilantro and green onion.

### Special Equipment

Large stock pot or Dutch oven, spider skimmer or slotted spoon, blender

### 
Make Ahead and Storage

Leftover papas guisadas can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 4 days or frozen for up to 3 months.
