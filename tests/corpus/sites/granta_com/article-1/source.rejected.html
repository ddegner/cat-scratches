Jeremy Atherton Lin

Jeremy Atherton Lin is an Asian-American essayist based in East Sussex, England. He has contributed to the Times Literary Supplement, the Yale Review, the Guardian, the Face, the White Review and GQ Style. His first book, Gay Bar (2021), received the National Book Critics Circle Award in Autobiography.

Publications

Gay Bar

Jeremy Atherton Lin

Brilliantly written and incisive’ Colm Tóibín
‘An absolute tour de force’ Maggie Nelson’

Propulsive music and euphoric crowds; drag queens and go-go dancers; strobe lights, dark rooms and glory holes. Gay bars have long been sites of joy and solidarity, sexual expression and activism. But around the world, they are closing.

Atherton Lin draws from his experiences of clubs, pubs and dives in London, San Francisco and Los Angeles – and a transatlantic romance that began late one restless night – to trace queer histories. An expansive and vivacious celebration of an institution, Gay Bar is also a stylish, intimate exploration of what these spaces mean, how they are changing and what we stand to lose when they close their doors.

‘Essential’ Vogue
‘This is exceptional writing’ Financial Times
